[Learn about the "What? Why? How?" strategy]

You can use the "What? Why? How?" strategy by asking yourself questions in the middle of trying to solve exercises. The questions are:

What are you doing or thinking right now?
Why are you doing it?
How well is it working for solving this problem?

Asking yourself these questions helps you better reflect on your thoughts.

In some exercises, you will be reminded to ask the questions because they will be in red text.

At first, you might find it unusual to ask yourself these questions, or not feel like you have a good answer. This is completely normal! These questions can still help you if you try to answer them – by typing, writing, and talking out loud or in your head. Eventually, you will ask yourself these questions automatically.
